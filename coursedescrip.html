Anatomy & Physiology

Our Anatomy and Physiology courses open exciting avenues for careers in the Health & Social Care Field


This course will allow you to: Identify and explain the 6 levels of structural organisations of the body Understand that the integumentary system is a organ system that protects the body from various types of damages Learn that the epidermis is the most superficial layer of the skin that covers almost the entire body surface Study the locomotive system and understand that it is an organ system that gives humans the ability to move using their muscular a skeletal systems Understand that the nervous system is a complex network of nerves and cells that carry messages to and from the brain and spinal cord to various parts of the body Identify the different organs that function the respiratory system and are responsible for taking in oxygen and expelling carbon dioxide Explore how the circulatory system permits blood to circulate around the body Understand how the immune system protects us against diseases Study the digestive system and how its organs work together to convert food into energy and basic nutrients to feed the entire body Explore the functions of the endocrine system and how it collects glands that produce hormones Develop knowledge about the reproductive system and explore both female and male functions Know the function and different parts of the urinary system, such as the kidneys, ureters, bladder, and the urethra The course will allow you to study whenever you want, at your own pace.
